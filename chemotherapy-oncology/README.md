# AI in Chemotherapy & Oncology

## Overview

AI is transforming cancer treatment through precision oncology — matching the right treatment to the right patient at the right time. From predicting drug response to optimizing dosing regimens and identifying new therapeutic targets, AI is accelerating every phase of the cancer care continuum.

**Market Size:** Precision oncology AI — $3.8B (2024) → projected $14.5B by 2030

## Challenges

1. **Tumor heterogeneity** — Cancers evolve and vary within a single patient
2. **Data silos** — Cancer data fragmented across hospitals
3. **Cost** — Comprehensive genomic profiling costs $3,000-5,000+
4. **Interpretation** — Variants of unknown significance (VUS) remain common
5. **Access equity** — Precision oncology primarily available in developed countries
6. **Resistance prediction** — Hard to predict when cancer will resist treatment
7. **Combination complexity** — Exponential drug combination possibilities
8. **Regulatory lag** — Companion diagnostic approvals take years
