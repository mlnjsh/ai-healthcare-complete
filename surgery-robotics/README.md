# AI in Surgery — Robotics & Automation

## Overview

AI-powered surgical robotics combines precision engineering with machine learning to enhance surgical procedures, reduce human error, and enable minimally invasive operations. The surgical robotics market is one of the fastest-growing segments in healthcare AI.

**Market Size:** $7.2B (2024) → projected $22.3B by 2030 (CAGR 20.7%)

## Autonomous Surgery Progress

### Levels of Surgical Autonomy (Adapted from Yang et al.)

| Level | Description | Current Status |
|-------|-------------|---------------|
| **Level 0** | No autonomy — manual surgery | Traditional surgery |
| **Level 1** | Robot provides assistance (tremor filtering, motion scaling) | da Vinci, Mako — **CURRENT STANDARD** |
| **Level 2** | Task autonomy (robot performs specific subtasks) | STAR suturing — **RESEARCH** |
| **Level 3** | Conditional autonomy (robot performs tasks with surgeon oversight) | **EMERGING RESEARCH** |
| **Level 4** | High autonomy (robot performs most of procedure) | **FUTURE (2030+)** |
| **Level 5** | Full autonomy (no human involvement) | **THEORETICAL** |

- **2006:** da Vinci becomes standard for prostatectomy

## Surgical Specialties & AI Applications

### Orthopedic Surgery
- **Joint replacement:** Mako (Stryker) uses CT-based 3D planning + robotic arm for precise bone cuts
- **Spine surgery:** ROSA (Zimmer Biomet), Mazor X (Medtronic) for pedicle screw placement
- **Accuracy improvement:** 1.5mm precision vs 3-5mm manual
- **Outcome data:** 30% fewer revisions with robotic-assisted knee replacement

### Cardiac Surgery
- **CABG:** Robot-assisted coronary bypass with da Vinci
- **Valve repair:** Minimally invasive mitral valve procedures
- **Ablation:** AI-guided cardiac ablation for arrhythmia
- **Planning:** AI-predicted optimal graft sites

### Neurosurgery
- **Brain tumor resection:** AI-guided navigation with intraoperative MRI
- **Deep brain stimulation:** Robot-assisted electrode placement (ROSA)
- **Epilepsy surgery:** SEEG electrode placement with robotic guidance
- **Spinal fusion:** AI-planned, robot-placed pedicle screws

### Urologic Surgery
- **Prostatectomy:** da Vinci standard of care (>90% in US)
- **Nephrectomy:** Robot-assisted kidney removal
- **Cystectomy:** Bladder cancer surgery with AI planning

### General / Abdominal Surgery
- **Cholecystectomy:** AI-enhanced critical view of safety identification
- **Colorectal:** Robot-assisted resections
- **Bariatric:** AI-planned gastric bypass/sleeve

## Challenges & Limitations

1. **Cost** — Robotic systems cost $1-3M + annual maintenance
2. **Haptic feedback** — Limited tactile sensation in current robots
3. **Training curve** — Surgeons need 50-150 cases for proficiency
4. **Data standardization** — No universal surgical video format
5. **Liability** — Unclear responsibility when AI assists/fails
6. **Connectivity** — Remote surgery requires ultra-low latency (5G/6G)
7. **Generalization** — AI models struggle with anatomical variation
8. **Battery of evidence** — Limited RCTs comparing robotic vs traditional

## Future Directions (2025-2035)

1. **Microsurgery robots** — Sub-millimeter precision for neural/vascular repair
2. **Swarm robotics** — Multiple small robots coordinating inside the body
3. **Remote surgery** — 5G-enabled telesurgery across continents
4. **Surgical foundation models** — Universal understanding of surgical procedures
5. **Soft robotics** — Flexible robots for delicate tissue manipulation
6. **Augmented reality surgery** — Real-time AI overlay during procedures
7. **Self-learning systems** — Robots that improve from each surgery
8. **Nano-surgical robots** — Cellular-level interventions
